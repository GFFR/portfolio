# Creative Direction: Gonçalo Ramalho · Online CV

**Concept codename:** OPERATOR MODE
**Date:** May 2026
**Format:** Single-page interactive HTML site

---

## The one-line thesis

> Your CV is a product. Your audience are product people. So ship a product, not a document.

## The concept

**OPERATOR MODE** treats the CV as a working operator's console — the kind of internal tool, dashboard, and AI-augmented workflow you actually build for the companies you join. Instead of *saying* you ship internal tools and AI workflows, the site itself **is** one. The medium is the message, and the message is: *I am the bridge between design, engineering, operations, and product — and here is the proof, in working form.*

This concept is uniquely defensible because almost no one else can credibly pull it off. It requires the exact stack of skills you have: designer-developer roots, product judgment, operational instincts, and now an AI-augmented building practice. Recruiters and CPOs reading this will recognize the move within five seconds — and that recognition itself is the hire pitch.

## Why this beats the inspiration set

The 26 portfolios in the sitebuilderreport list fall into three buckets: clean Squarespace minimalism, editorial-serif elegance, and Webflow-gradient storytelling. They are all *about* the person. None of them *are* the person's thesis in working form. By making the site demonstrate the work rather than describe it, you create a category of one.

## Visual language

- **Mood:** Dark mode console / quiet command center. Confident, alive, restrained.
- **Palette:** Deep slate `#0E1014`, near-white `#F4F1EA`, signal green `#3EE07F` accent (for "live"/"shipped" states), warm amber `#E8A85C` for editorial emphasis.
- **Typography:** Display serif for the editorial moments (name, manifesto, big quotes), monospace for system UI labels (`§ 03 · RELEASES`, status bars, timestamps), clean sans for body. The mix mirrors the bridge — left brain meets right brain.
- **Motion:** Quiet but alive. Pulsing status dot. Live Lisbon clock. Counters tick on reveal. Cards lift and reveal on hover. Smooth scroll. No gimmicks for gimmicks' sake.
- **Texture:** Faint grid background. Subtle ambient glows behind hero. Hairline borders. Real interactivity, not decoration.

## Narrative arc (seven movements)

1. **`§ 00 · BOOT`** — Hero. Name in massive serif, live status bar (time in Lisbon, "current build" = Avila Spaces, availability = selective engagements). One-line thesis: "I am the bridge."
2. **`§ 01 · README`** — Manifesto. The "fifteen years between design, engineering, and product" passage from your LinkedIn, rewritten tighter, with annotated highlights you can hover.
3. **`§ 02 · RELEASES`** — Career as versioned product releases. `v0.1 · Designer & Front-end` → `v6.0 · CIO`. Each release expandable with shipped scope, learnings, scale.
4. **`§ 03 · CASE FILES`** — Three deep dives: Brixel (zero-to-one), Gridwork (enterprise pivot), Avila Spaces (AI transformation). Each follows the same beat: *Ambiguity → Clarity → Execution → Outcome.* This is the structure of your career and your value prop.
5. **`§ 04 · TOOLKIT`** — Interactive capability matrix. Six domains (Strategy, Product, Operations, Engineering, Design, AI). Click to filter the case files by which capability they exercised.
6. **`§ 05 · SHIPPED FOR`** — Brand grid: Google, Schweppes, MTV, Nike, VW, Pandora, plus the Swiss banks/insurers from Brixel/Gridwork. Wordmark style, monochrome, animates on scroll.
7. **`§ 06 · CONSOLE`** — Contact section as a command palette / terminal. Real input field accepting commands (`email`, `linkedin`, `book`, `cv`, `help`). Also clickable for the non-developer audience. Closes the loop on the concept.

## Voice rules

- First person. Direct. No fluff.
- Lead with the verb: *I shipped, I built, I turned.*
- Real numbers and named outcomes whenever possible.
- Use your own line — "I am the bridge" — as the anchor.
- Stay specific: name the partners (banks, insurers), name the brands, name the platforms.
- Keep one line of dry humor in the console. Not more.

## Content blocks the site must include

- All seven roles from the LinkedIn (Avila, Gridwork, Brixel ×3, Wide Ventures ×3 collapsed, Spotshot, Activate)
- The brand list from Activate (Google, Schweppes, MTV, Nike, VW, Pandora)
- Education (Universidade Europeia — Mgmt & Business Strategy; ESAD — Graphic & Digital Design)
- Languages (EN, PT) and location (Lisbon)
- Contact (email, mobile, LinkedIn, personal site)

## What we deliberately drop from the existing mockup

- The cream/serif editorial-magazine aesthetic (beautiful but everywhere; doesn't differentiate)
- The `§ 04 · CAPABILITY` section as empty placeholder (becomes the live Toolkit)
- The italic-prose hero (replaced with a louder typographic moment + live status)

## What we keep from the existing mockup

- The bold serif typography for editorial moments
- The `§ NN · LABEL` section markers (they earn their keep in OPERATOR MODE)
- The numbered stats (15 years, etc.) — promoted into the live status bar

## Success criteria

1. A recruiter or CPO understands who you are and what you do within five seconds of landing.
2. They feel a pang of "I want to hire this person" before they finish reading.
3. The site itself becomes shareable proof of your craft.
